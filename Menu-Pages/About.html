About Me
& My Practice
My story starts with movement. Growing up abroad tuned my eye to the spaces where different cultures and perspectives meet. That same curiosity drives my design practice, where I explore the systems, ethics, and human connections that shape our world.

My work is grounded in a few core convictions.

I see systems as living networks. I am less interested in rigid frameworks and more in how visual and social structures can be made more adaptable, inclusive, and revealing of underlying relationships.

I am committed to sustainable and ethical outcomes. I believe in a design process that considers environmental impact, longevity, and social good, aiming to create work that is not only effective but also responsible.

I focus on human centered connections. At the heart of my practice is a drive to understand and serve the audience, using design to foster clarity, empathy, and meaningful engagement.

This leads to a practice that is inherently interdisciplinary and process led. I blend research, writing, and hands on making, allowing the questions I ask to guide the form of the work. For me, design is a tool for critical inquiry. Its ultimate purpose is to create intelligent, intentional, and positive outcomes.
